The Strait of Hormuz remains blocked to most maritime traffic, choking off ~20% of global crude supply. This directly caused the inflation spike from 2.4% (Feb) → 4.2% (May). With negotiations stalled, crude oil is likely to resume its upward trajectory, re-igniting headline inflation pressure. **Bottom line: the "supply-side disinflation" narrative from June's CPI drop may already be dead.**
