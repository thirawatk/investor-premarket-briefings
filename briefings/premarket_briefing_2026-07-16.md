## 3. Key Themes

### 🔥 **CPI BEAT: Best Inflation Print Since 2020 — Markets Breathe**
June CPI came in at 3.5% YoY, smashing the 3.8% consensus and dropping sharply from May's 4.2%. Month-over-month, prices actually *fell* 0.4% vs an expected 0.1% decline. Core inflation also cooled meaningfully. This is the single best inflation report since mid-2020 and immediately reset rate expectations. Bond yields tumbled (2Y down >7bp), the Nasdaq jumped 0.9%, and the growth-to-value rotation that had plagued tech all month finally reversed. But one month does not make a trend: the Fed has been burned by "transitory" before and Warsh's testimony made clear he's not ready to declare victory.

### ⚠️ **IBM CRASHES 25% — AI Spending Boom Has a Dark Side**
IBM issued a shocking Q2 profit warning on July 14, sending shares down 25% in a single session — the worst daily decline in the company's 115-year history. CEO Arvind Krishna explicitly blamed the AI infrastructure boom: "numerous large deals failed to close as expected" because enterprise customers are redirecting IT budgets toward GPU clusters and cloud AI capacity rather than traditional software and infrastructure. Revenue came in at $17.2B (+1% YoY), well below street estimates. This is a **Damodaran moment**: IBM is being disrupted by the very AI revolution it claims to serve. The stock is a value trap — optically cheap but structurally challenged. Full earnings July 22 will be critical. For broader markets, the question is: which other legacy enterprise tech names are quietly losing budget share to the AI capex tsunami?

### 🏦 **ASML RAISES GUIDANCE — AI Chip Demand Is Real**
ASML reported Q2 net sales of €9.3B with 54% gross margin, beating estimates, and raised full-year 2026 revenue guidance to €43-45B (from ~€40B prior). The Dutch lithography monopolist confirmed that demand for advanced chipmaking tools — driven by AI, 2nm process transitions, and Intel's foundry ambitions — remains robust. ASML is up ~60% YTD and this print validates the bull case. However, at ~35x forward earnings, the stock already prices in perfection. The Seeking Alpha Quant Rating has held steady at Hold, with valuation flagged as stretched.

### 🛢️ **STRAIT OF HORMUZ: Blockade Reinstated, Oil Holds Above $78**
President Trump reinstated the Strait of Hormuz blockade on July 13 after Iran attacked three more commercial vessels on July 6-7, declaring the June ceasefire "over." The following day he walked back talk of long-term military escalation, but the damage was done: Brent crude spiked 4% to $87 before settling, and WTI remains above $78. Roughly 20-25% of the world's seaborne oil trade transits the strait. While the immediate crisis appears contained (no major supply disruption), a single Iranian missile strike on a tanker could send crude above $100 overnight. This is the market's most dangerous tail risk right now. Energy stocks have been the week's best performers — but are you buying at the top of a geopolitical panic?

His core message: the Fed will bring inflation to 2% after five years of overshooting, and he will not "declare premature victory." The tone was hawkish — consistent with a man who wants to preserve optionality for a July 28-29 rate hike if data warrants. But the market isn't buying it. Warsh may talk tough, but the bond market is already pricing the pivot.

## 5. The Hard Truth — Damodaran Lens

*"If you cannot value a company, you should not own it. Period." — Aswath Damodaran*

Let's strip away the narratives and look at the numbers.

**The market is expensive by any historical measure.** The S&P 500 at 7,543 trades at approximately 22-23x forward earnings, the Shiller CAPE is well above 35, and the equity risk premium (ERP) — the excess return investors demand over the risk-free rate — is compressed to near 20-year lows. The implied ERP on the S&P 500, using a 4.57% risk-free rate and consensus earnings growth of ~8%, is roughly 3.5-4.0%. That is **razor thin**. You are being paid very little to take equity risk right now.

**IBM is a textbook value trap — and there will be more.** IBM trades at what looks like a cheap 12-13x earnings. But those earnings are declining in real terms, and the company's core software franchise is being hollowed out by AI-driven budget reallocation. A low P/E on deteriorating fundamentals is not a bargain — it's a warning. Every dollar of enterprise IT spend redirected to NVIDIA GPUs or cloud AI infrastructure is a dollar *not* spent on IBM's legacy software and services. The stock's 25% crash is the market finally acknowledging what the numbers have been saying for quarters. If you own Oracle (ORCL), SAP (SAP), or any legacy enterprise software name, ask yourself: is my company's moat wide enough to survive the AI budget reallocation?

**ASML is a genuine moat company — but price matters.** ASML has a near-monopoly on EUV lithography. That is a real competitive advantage. But at 35x forward earnings and +60% YTD, the stock is pricing in five years of uninterrupted growth. The Seeking Alpha Quant Rating (Hold) and Factor Grades flagging stretched valuation should give pause. If you must own it, wait for a 15-20% pullback. Buying at these levels means you need everything to go right — and in investing, things rarely go right for long.

**The oil risk premium is underpriced.** WTI at $78 implies the market assigns a low probability to a full Strait of Hormuz closure. But 20% of the world's seaborne oil transits that narrow channel, and Iran has attacked ships three times in 10 days. The probability of a supply disruption is low — maybe 10-15% — but the *impact* is catastrophic ($120+ oil). This is an asymmetric risk that options markets have not fully priced. If you have energy exposure in your portfolio, consider whether you truly understand the downside scenario.

**The CPI number was genuinely good — but it's one data point.** Inflation falling from 4.2% to 3.5% in a single month is meaningful. But the Fed's credibility is shot after five years of overshooting 2%. Warsh's testimony made clear he will not be the chair who "declared victory and then watched inflation reaccelerate." The Fed is data-dependent, but it is also reputation-dependent. A July rate hike is still ~40% priced. Do not get complacent.

**The small-cap rally has legs — with caveats.** Russell 2000 +19.5% YTD is driven by two things: (1) domestic-focused companies benefit less from dollar strength and trade war risk, and (2) falling rates reduce refinancing risk for heavily indebted small caps. Both narratives have merit. But small-cap earnings quality is systematically lower than large-cap, and a recession — if it comes — hits small caps hardest. The Damodaran approach: own small caps through a broad index (IWM), not through stock-picking. The margin of safety in individual small caps is too thin.

**Bottom line:** The market is bidding up risk assets on the hope of a soft landing, falling inflation, and an AI productivity boom. Hope is not an investment strategy. Know what you own, know what you paid, and know what it's worth. If you cannot answer all three, you are speculating, not investing.
