SECTION 3: KEY THEMES

================================================================================

--- 1. CHIP STOCK REBOUND: THE $1T BOUNCE --------------------------------------------------------

The semiconductor sector roared back Monday after a brutal $1 trillion selloff
last week. The SOXX ETF surged 3.9%. Intel (INTC) surged +11.82% on reports
Google will use its chipmaking services, while Micron (MU) jumped +10.30% and
Lam Research (LRCX) +8.54%. Nvidia (NVDA) +1.70%, AMD +5.29%. Jensen Huang
told investors they should be "very happy" -- but the bounce is happening
against a backdrop of an escalating Iran war, oil near $95, and Treasury
yields climbing. The snapback is technically-driven short-covering, not new
conviction. The sector was down ~15% from highs before this bounce. Don't
mistake a dead-cat bounce for a trend reversal.

--- 2. US-IRAN WAR: THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ FACTOR -----------------------------------------------

The war entered Day 102. Iran launched missiles at US bases in Kuwait and
Bahrain over the weekend, and the US struck Iranian tankers in the Strait of
Hormuz. Trump says both sides are "looking to agree an immediate truce" but
Iran simultaneously warned of "other fronts." The Strait -- through which
~20% of global oil and LNG flows -- remains effectively disrupted. The IEA
calls this the "largest supply disruption in history" with Gulf output cut by
14+ million bpd. WTI hit $95.47 intraday Monday before settling at $91.27.
Brent at $94.37. The Pentagon told Congress the first week alone cost $11.3
billion. This is the single biggest tail risk to the global economy, and the
market is pricing in a ceasefire that hasn't materialized.

--- 3. MAY CPI: TODAY'S MAIN EVENT ---------------------------------------------------------------

May CPI drops at 8:30 AM ET today. Consensus: 2.4% YoY headline, 2.5% core.
This is the most consequential data point since the war began because it
determines whether the Fed can cut at the June 17 FOMC. Right now, CME
FedWatch prices a 36% chance of a 25bp cut at the June meeting -- meaning
the base case is hold at 3.50-3.75%. But the market is forward-looking: if
CPI comes in at or below consensus, the cut probability rises and risk assets
rally. If it prints hot (2.6%+), the cut narrative collapses, yields spike,
and the equity rally reverses violently. Energy prices are the wild card --
oil at $95 feeds directly into headline inflation. The market is set up for
a binary outcome.

--- 4. TREASURY YIELDS: THE INVERSION THAT WASN'T -----------------------------------------------

The 10Y yield at 4.556% and the 30Y at 5.03% reflect a market that has
largely given up on near-term rate cuts. The 13-week T-bill at 3.628% shows
the front end is anchored by the Fed's current 3.50-3.75% target range. The
5Y at 4.289% suggests the market expects rates to stay elevated for years.
The 10Y-2Y spread has normalized (positive), but the absolute level of
long-end yields is a headwind for equity valuations. At 7,428, the S&P 500's
earnings yield is roughly 5.4% -- only ~50bps above the 10Y yield. That's
the thinnest equity risk premium in years and leaves no margin for error.

--- 5. GOLD: THE SILENT WARNING -------------------------------------------------------------------

Gold at $4,365/oz is down from its January all-time high of $5,608 but still
elevated by historical standards. The fact that gold is holding above $4,300
despite a strong dollar (DXY ~100) and rising real yields tells you something:
the market is hedging tail risks. Gold's 4% YTD decline from the ATH is
profit-taking, not a loss of conviction. Central bank buying (especially from
China, India, and emerging markets de-dollarizing) remains a structural
support. If CPI prints hot, gold could rally back toward $4,500 as an
inflation hedge. If the Iran ceasefire actually happens, gold loses its
geopolitical premium and could drop to $4,100-4,200.

--- 6. ECB: THE OTHER CENTRAL BANK ---------------------------------------------------------------

The European Central Bank is expected to raise its deposit rate by 25bps to
2.25% on Wednesday, June 11. Markets assign ~99% probability to this hike.
The ECB is fighting its own inflation battle, and the rate divergence between
the Fed (3.50-3.75%) and ECB (heading to 2.25%) is narrowing. EUR/USD at
~1.1550 reflects relative euro strength. If the ECB hikes and the Fed holds
(or cuts), the dollar could weaken further, which is marginally positive for
US equities but negative for US bond holders.

--- 7. SPACE IPO AND APPLE WWDC: DISTRACTION OR CATALYST? ----------------------------------------

SpaceX's IPO is reportedly "well oversubscribed" with orders closing
Wednesday. This is the most anticipated IPO in years and could suck liquidity
out of secondary markets. Meanwhile, Apple (AAPL) kicks off WWDC today with
a "second shot at its AI rollout" -- after the Siri/AI failures of 2025. AAPL
closed +2.01% Monday in anticipation. If Apple delivers a credible AI
narrative, it could re-rate the entire mega-cap tech complex. If it
disappoints, the "Magnificent 7" trade gets another leg of underperformance.

SECTION 5: THE HARD TRUTH -- DAMODARAN LENS

================================================================================

Let me be blunt about what the market is getting wrong.

**You are pricing a soft landing that hasn't happened yet.** The S&P 500 at
7,428 is within 2.5% of its all-time high of 7,620. The Nasdaq is up 1.27%
on the day. Chip stocks are bouncing 10-12% in a single session. This is not
the behavior of a market that understands the risks. This is the behavior of
a market that has been trained by 15 years of Fed put to buy every dip.

Here is what you are ignoring:

1. **Oil at $95 is inflationary. Period.** You cannot have crude at $95/bbl
   and expect core goods disinflation to continue. The CPI energy component
   will print hot today. And even if core CPI comes in at 2.5%, that is
   nowhere near the Fed's 2% target. The market is celebrating a number that
   is still 25bps above target as if it's a victory.

2. **The Iran war is not priced.** WTI at $95 implies a significant risk
   premium, but the S&P 500 is near ATHs. These two things cannot coexist
   indefinitely. If the Strait of Hormuz remains disrupted for another month,
   you are looking at $110-120 oil, which means recession. The market is
   pricing a ceasefire that Trump says is "being negotiated" but that Iran
   simultaneously denies. You are betting on hope, not fundamentals.

3. **The equity risk premium is broken.** At 7,428, the S&P 500 earnings yield
   is ~5.4%. The 10Y Treasury yields 4.56%. That's an equity risk premium of
   ~80bps. Historically, the ERP averages 300-400bps. You are being
   compensated almost nothing for taking equity risk. This is the definition
   of overvaluation.

4. **The Fed is not your friend.** The market prices a 36% chance of a June
   cut. That means 64% odds of NO cut. And even if they cut, it's 25bps
   from 3.75% to 3.50% -- still restrictive. The era of free money is over.
   The 10Y yield at 4.56% tells you the bond market doesn't believe in
   imminent easing. Stop listening to equity desks and start listening to the
   bond market.

5. **Chip stock euphoria is a trap.** Intel up 11.82% on a Google partnership
   rumor? Micron up 10% on nothing new? These are short squeezes, not
   fundamental re-ratings. The AI capex cycle is real, but the valuations
   were already pricing in perfection before the selloff. A 10% bounce from
   -15% drawdown does not make it cheap again.

**What to do:** If you are long, take some chips off the table into this
bounce. If CPI prints hot today, this rally reverses violently. If you are
looking to add risk, wait for the CPI print and the FOMC before making any
major allocation decisions. The next 7 days (CPI today, ECB Wednesday, PPI
Wednesday, FOMC June 17) will define the next quarter of market direction.
Do not front-run binary events with leveraged positions.
