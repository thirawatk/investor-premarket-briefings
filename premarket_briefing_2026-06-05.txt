SECTION 3: KEY THEMES
================================================================================

1. BROADCOM BLOWS UP THE AI TRADE (TEMPORARILY)
Broadcom (AVGO) crashed 14% on Thursday after missing Q2 revenue expectations
and maintaining its $100B AI chip sales forecast rather than raising it. The
selloff cascaded through the entire semiconductor complex -- Arm Holdings and
Micron both dropped over 5%, and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) fell 2%.
This is the first real crack in the AI trade since the March lows. The market
has been pricing in perfection from AI names, and Broadcom just showed that even
good isn't good enough when expectations are this elevated.

2. ROTATION FROM TECH TO VALUE/DEFENSIVE
The Dow hit a record high while the Nasdaq fell -- a textbook rotation. UnitedHealth
jumped 5%, JPMorgan climbed 4%, Walmart rose 1%, and Eli Lilly gained 5%. Investors
are moving out of crowded AI/tech positions and into banks, healthcare, and consumer
staples. This is healthy market behavior in the short term but raises questions about
whether the "narrow market" problem is being solved or just masked.

3. MIDDLE EAST ESCALATION AND OIL VOLATILITY
US-Iran tensions remain elevated. Iran struck Kuwait International Airport, and the
US carried out "self-defense strikes" on Qeshm Island. The Strait of Hormuz remains
effectively closed, threatening 20% of global oil supply. However, oil prices
retreated sharply on Thursday (WTI -4%, Brent -3%) as traders bet on a diplomatic
resolution. The oil market is now pricing in a ceasefire scenario. If talks fail,
the $100+ level comes back fast.

4. GOLD SURGES TO RECORDS ON INFLATION FEARS
Gold hit $4,509/oz, up nearly 1% on the day, driven by accelerating US inflation
and geopolitical hedging. The April PCE inflation print hit 3.8%, the highest since
2023. Markets are now pricing in a ~40% chance of at least one Fed rate hike before
year-end, a dramatic shift from three months ago when zero hikes were expected.
Gold is the clear beneficiary of this "stagflation-lite" environment.

5. BITCOIN IN FREE FALL
Bitcoin plunged 3.7% to ~$63,400, extending its decline from recent highs. The
crypto market is being squeezed by the stronger dollar, rising rate expectations,
and a broader risk-off sentiment in speculative assets. The correlation with Nasdaq
has reasserted itself after a brief decoupling.

6. KEVIN WARSH ERA BEGINS AT THE FED
The Senate confirmed Kevin Warsh as the 17th Fed Chair. His first FOMC meeting as
chair is June 16-17. Warsh is perceived as more hawkish than Powell, and his
arrival coincides with inflation re-accelerating. The blackout period for his
first meeting begins Saturday, June 6. Markets will scrutinize every word from
Fed speakers this week for clues about the new regime's reaction function.

7. JOBS FRIDAY LOOMS LARGE
Today's May nonfarm payrolls report is the main event. The April report showed
sharp gains following a strong March, and February's decline looks like an outlier.
Consensus expects another solid print. A hot number could reinforce rate hike
expectations and pressure bonds and tech stocks. A weak number could reignite
growth concerns but ease rate fears. Either way, expect volatility.

SECTION 5: THE HARD TRUTH -- DAMODARAN LENS
================================================================================

Let me be blunt about what's happening here:

THE MARKET IS PRICING IN PERFECTION AND CALLING IT A STRATEGY.
The S&P 500 is at 7,583 -- near record highs -- while inflation is
reaccelerating to 3.8%, the Fed is potentially hiking rates, the Middle East
is on fire, and the AI trade just had its first real stumble. The VIX at 15.5
is telling you the market thinks everything is fine. It's not.

BROADCOM IS A CANARY, NOT AN ISOLATED EVENT.
A 14% single-day drop in a mega-cap because guidance wasn't good enough tells
you the bar is impossibly high. The entire AI complex -- NVDA, AMD, MU, ARM --
is priced for 30-40% annual revenue growth sustained for years. One miss from
one company and the whole sector bleeds. What happens when the next one misses?
Or the next? You're not diversified if your "diversification" is 7 tech stocks
all trading at 30-50x earnings.

THE ROTATION IS A WARNING, NOT A BUY SIGNAL.
Money moving from tech into JPMorgan and UnitedHealth isn't bullish -- it's
defensive. Smart money is reducing risk into a jobs report that could go either
way, with a new Fed chair about to take the wheel, in the middle of a war.
If you're chasing the Dow's record high, ask yourself: are you buying strength,
or are you late to a rotation that's already happened?

OIL IS THE TAIL THAT WAGS THE DOG.
WTI at $92 is still historically elevated. The "diplomatic resolution" trade
that pulled oil off its highs is a bet on something that hasn't happened yet.
If the Strait of Hormuz stays closed for another two weeks, we're looking at
$100+ oil, which means higher inflation, which means the Fed hikes, which means
equities reprice lower. You're underestimating this risk.

THE FED PUT IS DEAD (FOR NOW).
Kevin Warsh is not Jerome Powell. He's not going to pivot at the first sign of
market stress. With inflation at 3.8% and rising, the Fed's hands are tied.
The market is pricing in a 40% chance of a hike by year-end. That's not priced
into equities. If that probability moves to 60-70%, the S&P 500 doesn't grind
higher -- it drops 5-8% in a week.

WHAT TO DO:
1. Reduce mega-cap tech exposure. Take profits in names that are up 30%+ YTD.
2. Add inflation protection: TIPS, commodities, gold miners.
3. Raise cash. 5-10% cash position gives you optionality if Jobs Friday
   triggers a volatility spike.
4. Hedge with VIX calls or put spreads on QQQ. The VIX is cheap at 15.5.
5. Don't chase the Dow. The rotation trade is already 2 days old.
