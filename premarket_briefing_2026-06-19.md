The framework includes U.S. sanctions relief, unfreezing of Iranian assets, and negotiations on a final deal within 60 days backed by a UN Security Council resolution. ## 5. The Hard Truth — Damodaran Lens

Let's strip away the euphoria.

**What actually changed this week?** Oil went from an inflated war price to a still-elevated peacetime price. The Fed confirmed it wants to *raise* rates, not cut them. And $6 trillion in options rolled off without blowing anything up. That's the raw material of this "rally."

**The peace deal is genuinely positive** — it removes a fat tail risk that had been distorting every asset price since the Iran war began. But ask yourself: did the S&P 500 at 7,500 suddenly become cheap? At 26x trailing earnings with the Fed signaling hikes, the equity risk premium is thinner than the Strait of Hormuz shipping lane. The oil crash cuts inflation, which is good for multiples — but the Fed is literally telling you they'll hike if inflation stays sticky, and the economy still has structural inflation (3.3% core PCE projected) baked in from the war period.

**The SeekingAlpha Quant data tells the real story:** Value and dividend factors have underperformed growth for months. The Quant system has Toyota as a Strong Buy and Tesla as a Hold — that's the market telling you the easy money in "buy the dip on everything" is done. Alpha Picks' +308% vs S&P's +84% is a reminder that stock selection matters enormously in this regime. The days of beta carrying everyone are over.

**The brutal question no one is asking:** If the peace deal takes oil from $105 to $74, and the Fed's dot plot still says "one hike," what happens when the next CPI print comes in *lower* because of the oil base effect? Warsh's hawkish stance could look very foolish very quickly — or inflation could stay sticky because the economy is running hot and the war merely masked it. The next two months of data (PCE Friday June 26, CPI July 10) will answer this. Betting on the direction now is gambling, not investing.

**Bottom line for the disciplined investor:** The peace deal is a sell-the-news event for oil, a buy-the-dip event for rate-sensitive stocks, and a profound uncertainty for the Fed's reaction function. The rational play is to take profits in Energy, add selectively to Tech (ADBE at 9x forward earnings is genuinely interesting per SeekingAlpha), and keep powder dry for the July FOMC meeting (July 29). The market just got what it wanted most — peace — but the hangover will be the realization that the Fed still has its finger on the rate-hike trigger.
