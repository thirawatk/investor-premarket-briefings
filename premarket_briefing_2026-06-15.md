## 5. The Hard Truth — Damodaran Lens

Let's cut through the euphoria.

**You're watching futures up 0.8% and calling it a rally.** No. VIX at 17.68 after a 9% drop is not "low volatility" — it's the calm after a 30-handle spike in April. The VIX is still above its historical median of ~17.5. This is normalization, not complacency.

**SpaceX IPO is not a market signal.** One company — even one as hyped as SpaceX — does not change the macro regime. The S&P 500 is priced at ~21x forward earnings with a 4.5% 10-year yield competing for your capital. The equity risk premium is thin, possibly negative. Every point of multiple expansion from here requires either lower rates or better earnings, and you're getting neither in the near term — the Fed is done cutting and Q2 earnings guidance has been muted.

**The peace trade is real, but priced.** Oil at $81 already reflects a 20% drop in a month. That's a lot of good news baked in. If peace talks stumble — and Iran is playing hard to get — oil snaps back to $90+ and equities sell off 2-3% in a session. You're being paid for the binary outcome right now, not the fundamentals.

**The real question nobody's asking:** Why is gold at $4,307 — a record — if peace is breaking out everywhere? Gold doesn't rally 23% YoY because the world is getting safer. It rallies because the monetary debasement trade is intact and the dollar's reserve status is eroding. The gold price is screaming something the equity VIX isn't listening to: structural instability in the fiat system.

**Your portfolio has a complacency problem if you're leaning hard into this rally without hedging.** The FOMC dot plot this Wednesday is the highest-conviction risk event of the month. A hawkish surprise — even one hike indicated for 2026 — could wipe out two weeks of gains in one afternoon. If you're not positioned for that asymmetric downside, you're not investing — you're hoping.
