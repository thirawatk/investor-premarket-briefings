3. KEY THEMES
================================================================================

**Theme 1: Manufacturing Roars Back**
The ISM Manufacturing PMI surprised dramatically at 54.0 in May, up from 52.7
in April and well above the 53.0 consensus. This is the highest reading since
May 2022 (55.9). The details were strong: output at a four-year high, new
orders expanding, and employment improving. The S&P Global manufacturing PMI
confirmed the strength at 55.1. This matters because manufacturing has been
in contraction territory for most of 2024-25, and a sustained rebound could
reignite cyclical inflation fears -- which complicates the rate-cut narrative.

**Theme 2: New Fed Chair, Same Dilemma**
Kevin Warsh is now Fed Chair, with his first FOMC meeting on June 16-17.
Markets price a 0% chance of a cut in June, but a ~36% chance in July
(Polymarket). The dilemma: Trump wants cuts aggressively, but April CPI data
showed shelter inflation doubled, and oil is trading near $90. Warsh faces a
hawkish committee that will resist political pressure. 24/7 Wall St. has
reported Warsh may actually need to hike, not cut. This is the central
uncertainty driving rate expectations.

**Theme 3: The AI Earnings Engine Keeps Running**
The Nasdaq gained approximately 8% in May and the S&P 500 rose ~5%, making
May the best month in a year. Dell surged 32.8% in a single session on AI
demand. Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, and the mega-cap tech complex continue to
lead breadth-challenged but conviction-strong rallies. The S&P 500 recorded
its ninth consecutive weekly gain -- the longest streak since 2023. AI
capex is the dominant thematic driver, and any crack in earnings would cause
severe pain given concentrated positioning.

**Theme 4: Oil Volatility and Tariff Fallout**
WTI crude surged 2.8% on June 1 to $89.82 after plummeting 19% in May -- its
worst month since March 2020. This whipsaw reflects supply uncertainty,
geopolitical risk, and demand anxiety tied to the tariff war. The Tax Foundation
estimates Trump's 2026 tariffs amount to a $700/household average tax increase.
US-China trade tensions persist despite recent diplomatic outreach. Eight
European countries were hit with new tariffs over the Greenland dispute.
Expect commodity volatility to remain elevated.

**Theme 5: Gold at $4,390 -- What the Bond Market Isn't Saying**
Gold has surged past $4,390/oz, setting fresh all-time highs. It's up roughly
25% year-to-date. This is not just a weak-dollar trade. Central banks -- led
by China, India, and Turkey -- have been buying gold at record pace, reducing
dollar reserve holdings. Simultaneously, US fiscal deficits remain wide (~$2T+
annually), and real yields, while positive, haven't deterred gold buyers.
Damodaran would note: gold at this level priced in a monetary regime change or
a significant loss of confidence in fiat stewardship. The bond market (10Y at
4.45%) disagrees, placing gold and bonds in a historically unusual tandem
rally. One of them is wrong.

**Theme 6: Credit Markets Stay Complacent**
High yield spreads sit at a tight 2.72% -- barely above cycle lows --
despite policy uncertainty, tariff shocks, and near-90 oil. Investment-grade
credit is even more compressed. This tells us risk appetite is robust but also
fragile. When spreads are this tight, the marginal buyer is fully allocated,
and any negative growth shock triggers rapid repricing. Monitor this as your
early-warning signal. A breakout above 3.50% in HY spreads would be the first
sign the equity rally is losing its credit market foundation.

5. THE HARD TRUTH -- DAMODARAN LENS
================================================================================

Let me be blunt about what's really going on and what most commentary is
missing.

**You priced in a perfect landing. The market is at all-time highs while oil
nearly hit $90, tariffs are the highest since WWII, and the 10-year yield
is at 4.45%.** This is not the setup for easy monetary policy. The AI
earnings concentration risk in the S&P 500 is the highest in history -- the
top 10 stocks now drive ~35% of the index's value. Dell popping 33% in a day
on "AI demand" exactly mirrors the telecom infrastructure bubble of 1999.

Damodaran's valuation framework at current rates (10Y = 4.45%, equity risk
premium ~3.5-4.0%) gives a fair-value S&P 500 in the 5,800-6,200 range
using normalized earnings and mid-cycle growth. The market is trading at
7,580. That is a 20-25% premium to "fair value" by conservative assumptions.

The bulls will say "AI changes everything and discount rates will fall."
Both are half-true at best. AI capex is real and earnings are growing, but
you're paying 30x forward earnings for those earnings while the cost of
capital is 4.5%. The math gets brutal fast if growth doesn't land exactly
as scripted. And Warsh cutting rates to placate Trump would be even more
dangerous -- it would blow up the long end and widen term premiums.

**The honest risk:** You're not being compensated for geopolitical risk
(Taiwan Strait tensions remain elevated), policy risk (tariff escalation),
and concentration risk (magnificent seven dependence). High-yield spreads at
2.72% are telling you the market is pricing in a goldilocks certainty it
doesn't have.

**What to do:** If you're long, maintain trailing stops. The setup is
more vulnerable to a 5-8% correction than it has been in months. A
hot JOLTS print + sticky PCE later this week would force a repricing of
the "rate cuts are coming" narrative. Watch VIX -- it's at 16, dangerously
low for the amount of uncertainty on the tape. A vol spike is the most
probable catalyst for a correction.
