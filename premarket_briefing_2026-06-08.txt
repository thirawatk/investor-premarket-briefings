SECTION 3: KEY THEMES
================================================================================

**1. JOBS SHOCK RESETS RATE EXPECTATIONS**
Friday's nonfarm payrolls printed at 172,000 -- more than double the 85,000
consensus -- while unemployment held at 4.3%. The result crushed the
narrative of a softening labor market that had been fueling rate-cut bets.
CME FedWatch now prices a 98.4% probability of a June hold, with the first
cut not fully priced until December. The 2-year yield jumped 10 bps to 4.15%,
and the market is now debating whether the Fed might need to HIKE rather than
cut. This is a material regime shift from just two weeks ago.

**2. TECH/AI SELLOFF ACCELERATES**
The Nasdaq's 4.2% Friday plunge was its worst single-day drop in months.
Nvidia, the poster child of the AI trade, saw its market cap hover near $4.97
trillion but is under pressure as investors question whether AI capex
justification can survive a higher-for-longer rate environment. The
"magnificent seven" cohort is now down significantly from February highs.
Alphabet is dipping in premarket on AI spending concerns and antitrust
pressure. The rotation out of momentum/growth into value/defensive is
accelerating.

**3. US-IRAN WAR & THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ CRISIS**
The US-Israel coalition conflict with Iran has effectively blocked the Strait
of Hormuz since February 28, disrupting roughly 20% of global oil transit.
WTI crude has pulled back from recent highs near $98 to ~$90.54 on
de-escalation hopes, but the structural supply risk remains. Iran has laid
fewer than 10 mines but has struck an oil tanker and launched missiles toward
Kuwait and Bahrain. Even a peace deal won't immediately restore full shipping
flows. This is the single largest geopolitical risk to global markets in 2026.

**4. SURGING TREASURY YIELDS & THE BOND SELL-OFF**
The 10-year yield has risen to 4.52%, with the 2-year at 4.15%. The curve
has steepened as the market prices in fewer cuts and potentially higher
terminal rates. The WSJ Dollar Index rose 1.07% on the week to 96.60. The
combination of strong jobs, sticky inflation expectations, and massive
government borrowing (the tax cut bill is coming) is creating a perfect storm
for bonds. TLT (20+ year Treasury ETF) is under severe pressure.

**5. COMMODITY DEFLATION FLASH -- GOLD & COPPER SLIDE**
Gold dropped below $4,420/oz on Friday, down over 3% for the week, as real
yields surged and the dollar strengthened. Copper plunged 3.8% to $6.26/lb on
growth concerns and the strong dollar. This is notable because copper is
often seen as a barometer of global economic health. The commodity complex is
sending mixed signals: oil is elevated on supply risk, but industrial metals
and precious metals are selling off on demand/dollar concerns.

**6. CHINA'S XI VISITS NORTH KOREA -- GEOPOLITICAL REALIGNMENT**
Xi Jinping arrives in Pyongyang today (June 8) for a two-day state visit --
his first trip to North Korea in nearly seven years and his first overseas
trip of 2026. This follows separate summits with Trump and Putin, signaling
Beijing's intent to reassert its alliance with Pyongyang amid deepening
US-China tensions. The visit underscores the formation of a China-Russia-North
Korea axis that has profound implications for Asian security, supply chains,
and the US dollar's role in the region.

**7. SPACEX IPO LOOMS -- JUNE 12 NASDAQ DEBUT**
SpaceX is targeting a Nasdaq debut on June 12 under ticker SPCX, with a
staggering valuation target of $1.77 trillion. The company generated $18.7
billion in 2025 revenue (+33% YoY). This will be the largest IPO in history
and could make Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire. The IPO market
hasn't seen anything like this, and the debut will be a major sentiment test
for high-growth/tech valuations in a rising rate environment.

SECTION 5: THE HARD TRUTH -- DAMODARAN LENS
================================================================================

Let me be brutally honest about where we are:

**The market has been living in a fantasy.** For nine consecutive weeks,
stocks climbed on the twin narratives of AI-driven growth and imminent Fed
cuts. Friday's reality check was overdue. The labor market is NOT weakening
-- it's resilient at 4.3% unemployment. Inflation is NOT conquered -- core
PCE is still running hot, and this week's CPI could confirm sticky price
pressures. The idea that the Fed would cut rates 2-3 times in 2026 was always
wishful thinking, and the market is now pricing what the data actually says:
NO cuts for the foreseeable future.

**The AI trade is facing its first real stress test.** For two years, the
narrative was "AI will justify any valuation." But in a world where the
10-year yield is 4.5%+ and the Fed is not cutting, the present value of
distant future AI profits drops materially. Nvidia at 35x forward earnings
is NOT cheap when risk-free rates are 4.5%. The market is starting to
discipline growth stock valuations, and there's more pain ahead for
overcrowded AI longs.

**The US-Iran war is an underpriced tail risk.** WTI at $90 assumes a
relatively quick resolution. But Iran has blocked the Strait of Hormuz for
three months with no resolution in sight. If the conflict escalates or
persists through summer, $120+ oil is plausible, which would be
recessionary and inflationary simultaneously -- the worst possible
combination for equities. The market is pricing this as a transient shock.
It might not be.

**The bond market is telling you something stocks refuse to hear.** With the
10-year at 4.52% and rising, the bond market is saying that fiscal
profligacy (the tax cut bill), persistent inflation, and strong growth mean
higher rates for longer. Equities are ignoring this at their peril. The
equity risk premium is compressing, and at some point, the arbitrage
between risk-free 4.5% and equity returns becomes a headwind, not a tailwind.

**What to do:** This is not the time to be heroic. Reduce beta. Trim AI/tech
overweights. Add duration only if you believe the Fed will be forced to cut
recession (not yet). Hold some cash. The next two weeks -- CPI on Wednesday,
FOMC on June 17-18 -- will determine whether this is a buying opportunity
or the beginning of a meaningful correction. Don't guess. Wait for the data.
