**Here's what the market is ignoring:**

1. **The Fed pivot narrative is dead.** A month ago, the story was "six cuts in 2026." Now the CME FedWatch shows a 37% probability of a July *hike*. If the Fed hikes, the equity risk premium compresses further, and the DCF math on every growth stock gets uglier. The AI capex story requires cheap capital — the exact opposite of what a hiking Fed delivers.

2. **Nike is a consumer proxy hiding in plain sight.** Nike's earnings tonight aren't just about sneakers. Flat revenue, cautious guidance, or China weakness would confirm what the macro data is whispering: the US consumer — 70% of GDP — is slowing. Consumer confidence data at 10 AM adds another data point. If both miss, the consumer discretionary rally of Monday evaporates.

3. **The Iran "peace dividend" is already priced.** Oil at $70.89 is back to pre-conflict levels. The incremental positive from here is marginal. What's not priced: Iran compliance risk, the multi-month timeline to clear the Strait of ordnance, and the geopolitical reality that the underlying US-Iran animosity hasn't changed one bit.

4. **This week's data gauntlet is asymmetric.** Thin holiday liquidity + ISM Wednesday + NFP Thursday = a volatility recipe. The market is pricing calm (VIX at 17.83). If ISM or NFP surprise meaningfully in either direction, the move will be magnified by absent participants. The VIX is your cheap hedge.

**Bottom line:** Enjoy the relief rally. The Iran deal is genuinely good news. But at 22x forward earnings with a Fed contemplating hikes and a consumer showing cracks, the risk/reward entering H2 is the worst it's been all year. If you're a value investor, you're not chasing this — you're trimming into strength and building a shopping list for when the H2 correction that SeekingAlpha is warning about actually arrives. **The time to buy is when the VIX is at 25, not 17.**
