## 3. Key Themes

### 🕊️ **US-Iran Peace Deal Reshapes the Macro Landscape**
The 60-day ceasefire signed Sunday and celebrated in Monday's session is the dominant narrative. The Strait of Hormuz — carrying 20% of global oil — reopens toll-free, and the US naval blockade of Iranian ports is lifted. This is a genuine structural shift: the war-driven inflation impulse that had been spiking since March is reversing. Oil crashed 5%+ on Monday alone; Brent fell from $115+ war highs to $83. The relief is being priced into equities, particularly rate-sensitive tech and consumer stocks. Goldman, CNBC, and SeekingAlpha all characterized this as a "hopium rally," but the economic reality of lower oil = lower input costs = lower inflation = less hawkish Fed is difficult to dismiss. The question is whether euphoria has overshot the reality — full recovery of Hormuz shipping and Iranian oil supply will take months.

### 🏛️ **Kevin Warsh's First FOMC — The Most Important Event of the Week**
The two-day FOMC meeting begins today with the decision Wednesday at 2:00 PM ET. Chair Kevin Warsh — a Trump appointee and former Fed Governor — takes the podium for his debut press conference. The rate decision is a near-certain hold (98% probability), but Warsh has telegraphed a shift away from forward guidance and the dot plot, which creates massive uncertainty. The May minutes showed an 8-4 vote to hold and a "majority" stressing that a rate hike may be needed this year. But the Iran peace deal and collapsing oil prices have dramatically changed the inflation outlook since those minutes. **The hard question:** Does Warsh use this meeting to pivot dovishly (lower oil = easing inflation = no need to hike) or hawkishly (inflation is still sticky at 3%+, and he wants to establish credibility early)? Markets are pricing no cuts and a potential hike by year-end. His tone will be the most-watched aspect.

### 🤝 **M&A Supercycle: Fox-Roku, Salesforce-Fin, Nuvei-Payoneer**
Monday was a banner day for dealmaking. Fox Corp buying Roku for $22B ($160/share: $96 cash + 0.9693 Fox shares) is a transformative bet on pairing Fox's sports/news with Roku's 80M+ streaming households. Salesforce acquiring Fin for $3.6B extends its AI-agent integration push. Nuvei buying Payoneer for $2.75B creates a cross-border payments giant. This wave of M&A signals CEO confidence returning after the Iran war uncertainty. It's a bullish signal for risk appetite and suggests corporate balance sheets remain healthy.

### 📉 **Analyst Rotation: Accenture Downgraded, Datadog/Rocket Lab Upgraded**
Morgan Stanley downgraded **Accenture (ACN)** to Equal Weight from Overweight, slashing its target from $240 to $177 — citing that "AI spending rationalization has not played out" and IT budget growth remains weak. Meanwhile, **Truist upgraded Datadog (DDOG)** to Buy (PT $300 from $190), seeing "continued upside momentum in observability." **KeyBanc upgraded Rocket Lab (RKLB)** to Overweight at $135 target. This is a clear rotation: sell legacy IT consulting (which faces headwinds from AI disruption and cautious enterprise budgets), buy digital-native infrastructure plays that benefit from AI/hyperscaler spending. DDOG shares rose 2.7% premarket on Monday.

### 🛢️ **Oil Crash: The Inflation Panacea the Fed Needed**
WTI crude at $80 is down over 30% from war peaks and at 3-month lows. The US Strategic Petroleum Reserve is at its lowest since the early 1980s. But the peace deal removes the supply risk premium that had been embedded in oil since March. If oil stabilizes in the $75-85 range heading into July, headline CPI (which had been re-accelerating) will likely stall or decline. This gives the Fed cover to hold without hiking. The market is pricing this correctly, but watch for the lag: lower oil takes 3-6 months to feed through to CPI.

### 🚀 **SpaceX IPO Hangover: $2 Trillion Milestone Reshapes Tech**
SpaceX went public last Friday (June 12) in a historic IPO, closing up 19% in its debut and making Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire. The stock (SPCX) has been a major driver of Nasdaq sentiment, dragging the index to new highs alongside the Iran relief rally. Analysts are now debating index inclusion, options listing, and whether the $2T+ valuation is sustainable. Barron's called it "The Great, Big, Boring IPO" — suggesting the absence of chaos was itself a bullish signal.

### 🇯🇵 **BOJ Decision Looms — Parallel Risk for Global Rates**
The Bank of Japan meets this week alongside the Fed and is expected to raise rates to a 31-year high (likely 0.75%-1.00%). This matters because higher Japanese rates = yen strengthening = potential unwind of the massive yen carry trade that has been funding risk asset purchases globally. The USD/JPY at ~160 is near multi-decade lows for the yen. A BOJ hike could trigger a sharp reversal in risk appetite globally, especially if the Fed tone is simultaneously hawkish.

## 5. The Hard Truth — Damodaran Lens

**Stop pretending this rally is skill. It's a 100% exogenous shock — and you need to be honest about what that means for your portfolio.**

The US-Iran peace deal is the kind of event where everyone who owned stocks from March through last Friday was effectively short a war premium they never consciously priced in. If you held risk assets through the Iran conflict, you were not "right" — you were lucky that the geopolitical gamble paid off in your favor. And if you sold into the war panic, you were not "wrong" — you correctly priced a risk that, in most historical playbooks, would have lasted much longer.

**Here's the uncomfortable truth from a value perspective:** The S&P 500 at 7,569 and Nasdaq at 26,647 are not cheap by any traditional measure. The Shiller CAPE ratio is north of 35. The market cap-to-GDP ratio is near all-time highs. Even after the Iran war shock temporarily compressed multiples, we're right back to pricing in perfection. The peace deal doesn't suddenly make Nvidia worth more or make 3% inflation acceptable — it removes a tail risk that should never have been the primary basis for your asset allocation in the first place.

**The Damodaran framework would ask three questions right now:**

1. **What is the intrinsic value of the S&P 500 today?** At 7,569 with earnings around $250/share, the forward P/E is ~30x. A 10-year yield at 4.45% implies an equity risk premium of roughly 1.5-2.0% — historically thin. You're paying for perfection on the earnings front, and the Iran deal doesn't change the structural challenges: AI capex ROI uncertainty, deglobalization pressure on margins, and a Fed that's pivoted from "maybe cuts" to "maybe hikes."

2. **Does the peace deal change the fundamental earnings trajectory?** Lower oil helps airlines, transport, and consumer discretionary — call it a 3-5% EPS tailwind for the broad index. But that's a one-time reset, not a sustainable growth driver. The real question for Q3-Q4 earnings is whether AI-driven productivity gains are materializing in corporate margins. If they aren't, the 2026 earnings consensus of $275-285/share is too high.

3. **What's the margin of safety?** There is none at current levels. The VIX at 16 tells you the options market agrees — volatility has collapsed, but that itself is a warning sign. When the VIX drops below 15, it often signals complacency rather than genuine stability. A hawkish Warsh surprise on Wednesday or a BOJ rate hike that triggers carry trade unwinds could reverse this entire rally in 48 hours.

**The hard advice:** If you're sitting on significant YTD gains from holding through the war, take at least some off the table here. Tax-loss harvest if you have losers. But don't get seduced into thinking the peace deal is a "new paradigm." It's a return to the pre-war status quo — which was already overvalued and fragile. Use this liquidity event to rebalance toward quality, not to chase momentum into AI stocks that have doubled on hype.
